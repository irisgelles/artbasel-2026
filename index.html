standout works from 2025 — verified

Andy Warhol — Muhammad Ali (1977)
Lévy Gorvy Dayan · Sold $18,000,000
Acrylic and silkscreen on canvas, signed by Ali on the reverse. The highest reported sale of the entire fair — and shown at the exact building where Ali defeated Sonny Liston in 1964. Sold on VIP day.
Yayoi Kusama — Where the Lights in My Heart Go (2016)
Victoria Miro Gallery
One of only six infinity mirror rooms that use natural light instead of LEDs. The line wrapped around the booth all day. About obsession, self-erasure, and the night sky — one of the 10 artists on your study list.
Betye Saar — Seeking the Promise (2025)
Roberts Projects · Kabinett Sector · Art Basel Awards Medalist
A vintage upright rowboat filled with antlers, a clock, carved shapes and found objects — a shrine about ancestral memory and spiritual journey. Saar was 99. She turns 100 in 2026.

Beeple — Regular Animals (2025)
Zero 10 Digital Sector · $100,000 each · Sold out on VIP day
Six robot dogs with hyper-realistic heads of Musk, Zuckerberg, Bezos, Warhol, Picasso — and Beeple himself. They roam, photograph visitors with AI, then "poop" printed NFTs. The most talked-about installation of the fair.

All four works verified across multiple independent sources including Art Basel, Artsy, The Art Newspaper, CNN, and Art & Object.
